The Beginning

Blayden is accelerating. The volume is up, the tempo is climbing, and there is no pause between ideas. Riffs lock in and surge forward. Melodies cut through with urgency. Traditional metal burns at the core, but it is driven harder now, sharpened by speed and aggression that demand movement. Vocals rise and tear through the noise. Guitars spiral and collide. Bass and drums hit like a heartbeat pushed past comfort. This is not chaos. This is pressure applied with intent.

Momentum is building fast. Skills are being pushed to their limits. Songs are forged under repetition and strain, stripped down until only what works survives. Shows are lining up, each one another test at full volume. The first EP is taking shape in real time, fueled by discipline, obsession, and refusal to slow down. Songwriting remains the anchor. Depth over flash. Purpose over excess. This is the phase where everything sharpens, where motion becomes direction. Blayden is moving forward, fast, and there is no plan to ease off.
